Scenario 2: General Instructions



You are a self-employed person.

Your initial capital or savings until now is $10,000.

Your quarterly earnings are $3000

You have bought health-insurance. Each quarter (every 3 months) you pay $500 to insurance as insurance premium.

Hence your quartely savings are $2500 after paying for premium.

E.g. your Cumulative Economic Value (CEV) at end of first quarter will be $10,000 + $2,500 = $12,500.

Your goal is to maximise your Cumulative Economic Value (CEV) by end of 3 years. If you fall sick, then you have two options:

1. Status quo (do nothing): Then illness depcreciates/reduces your cumulative economic value by the amount of Cost of Illness;

2. Seek healthcare: If you seek health-care then it eliminates the Cost of Illness and you pay for cost of health-care and time-cost: The cost of healthcare is indicated by Cost of Care. Seeking care has additional time cost indicated by number or hours. Each hour of care costs $10.

- Your insurance will pay for healthcare until a annual $75 deductible. Thereafter, you will pay 20% of cost, while your insurance will pay 80%.

- E.g. If your cost of care was $100, then you pay the deductible of $75. Remainder is $100 - $75 = $25. You pay 20% of this remainder: 20% x $25 = $5. Your total cost = $75 + $5 = $80.

- If you exhausted or reached your deductible, then you only pay for co-pay until end of year. At end of each year the deductible will reset to $75.

In middle of each year, you have the option of getting an annual examination. The cost of examination and time cost is indicated. Deductible and co-pay apply to this too.

Make your decision at point and maximise your CEV
