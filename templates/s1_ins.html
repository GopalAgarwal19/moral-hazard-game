Scenario 1: General Instructions



You are a self-employed person.

Your initial capital or savings until now is $10,000.

Your quarterly earnings are $3000

This means, every 3 months you get this income and it will increase your savings by $3000

E.g. your Cumulative Economic Value (CEV) at end of first quarter will be $10,000 + $3,000 = $13,000.

Your goal is to maximise your Cumulative Economic Value (CEV) by end of 3 years. If you fall sick, then you have two options:

1. Status quo (do nothing): Then illness depcreciates/reduces your cumulative economic value by the amount of Cost of Illness;

2. Seek healthcare: The cost of healthcare is indicated by Cost of Care. Seeking care has additional time cost indicated by number or hours. Each hour of care costs $10. Seeking healthcare reduces your cumulatives economic value by [Cost of Care + Time Cost].

Thus, you either reduce CEV by [Cost of Illness] or by [Cost of Care + Time Cost]

In middle of each year, you have the option of getting an annual examination. The cost of examination and time cost is indicated. Your CEV will be reduced by [Cost of Examination + Time Cost]

Make your decision at point and maximise your CEV
